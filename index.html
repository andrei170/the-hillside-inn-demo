Heritage & Credential Authority

An 1896 building on the National Register of Historic Places. A four-room log cabin origin. A 2002 renovation. A 2016 reopening.

National Register listing is one of the strongest, most easily verified credentials a U.S. lodging property can hold. It is also one of the most commonly missing from a hotel's own structured data. That is the central asymmetry this audit identifies for The Hillside Inn.

What we would build

A structured-data stack that gives the engines exactly what they are looking for · in the precise format they ingest.

Every item below is a discrete, verifiable component. Together they convert the credential stack already true about The Hillside into the credential stack the engines actually read. No paid traffic. No copy rewrite. No marketing posture change. Only the structured surface beneath the existing site.

Person Schema

Diane Taillon · Person schema with full multi-career arc

A complete Person record that names Niagara Falls origin, SUNY Buffalo, Peace Corps Kenya, 22-year medical practice, Washington D.C. relocation, real estate entry, Sotheby's International Realty · Door County affiliation, Arbor Crowne Properties founding, and Hillside reopening. This is the single highest-leverage item in the build.

Lodging Schema Stack

BedAndBreakfast + LodgingBusiness + Hotel triple-tagged on the root

Engines disagree on which lodging vocabulary to read first. We tag the root entity with all three so that no matter which schema the engine prefers, The Hillside is correctly identified as a luxury, owner-operated, historic inn.

RealEstateAgent

Arbor Crowne Properties · cross-linked RealEstateAgent schema

The dual-business reality (brokerage operating from the inn's own lobby) is rare and credible. A separate RealEstateAgent entity for Arbor Crowne is published, with sameAs / parentOrganization links binding it to Diane Taillon and to The Hillside Inn · making the dual-business fact machine-readable.

NRHP Credential

Dedicated National Register credential authority page

A single, indexable page that states the 1896 origin, the four-room log cabin origin, the 2002 renovation, the 2016 reopening, and the NRHP listing in structured language engines can lift verbatim. Linked from every suite and cottage page.

Per-Suite Schema

Seven HotelRoom / Suite entities · five named suites + two cottages

Forget-Me-Not, Marigold, Day Lily, Mayflower, Trillium, North Cottage, South Cottage · each gets its own machine-readable HotelRoom or Suite record with bedroom/bath specs for the cottages and named-suite identity for the five inn rooms. This turns each unit into a separately discoverable entity.

LocalBusiness

9980 Water Street · LocalBusiness + PostalAddress + GeoCoordinates

Address, geographic coordinates, and contact data structured for ingestion by map-aware engines and AI assistants. The "Ephraim, Door County" geographic identity is reinforced at the entity level rather than only in prose.

FAQPage

FAQPage schema covering the five queries this audit identified

Each of the five queries currently returning zero citations becomes an FAQ on the site, paired with an FAQPage record. This is one of the most direct mechanisms by which AI engines lift a property into a cited answer.

Niche Capture

"Only luxury inn on the bay in Northern Door County" capture page

An indexable page built around the exact phrase that defines the property's niche. Linked from the homepage, mirrored in structured data, and surfaced in the FAQ · so the literal claim becomes the literal answer the engines return.

How is this different from SEO?
Traditional SEO targets blue-link results · the ten classic search listings under a query. This audit targets AI engine answers · the paragraph an AI assistant writes when a traveler asks about Ephraim's best waterfront inn. The mechanisms are related but distinct. AI engines lean heavily on structured data, sameAs links between entities, FAQPage records, and explicit credential schemas. A hotel with strong SEO can still be invisible to AI engines if the structured surface is missing · which is the exact pattern The Hillside currently presents.
Will any of this require changing the look, voice, or booking flow of the existing site?
No. The work is structural, not visual. The deliverables in the build list operate beneath the surface the guest sees. The published prose, the photography, the booking pathway, and the brand voice all remain owned by the inn. What changes is what the machines read · not what the human reader sees.
Why is the NRHP listing such a load-bearing credential?
National Register listings are independently verifiable, stable over time, and rare among boutique hotels. AI engines weight credentials that are externally validated and stable far more heavily than self-described ones. The 1896 NRHP listing is one of the strongest signals the inn can carry into a structured profile · which makes its current absence from machine-readable surface area the single most cost-efficient fix in the audit.
What happens to the Arbor Crowne brokerage in the build?
Arbor Crowne Properties is treated as a separate but cross-linked entity. It gets its own RealEstateAgent record, its own credential surface, and explicit sameAs / parentOrganization links binding it to Diane Taillon and to The Hillside Inn. The dual-business fact · that a top-of-market Door County brokerage operates from the lobby of an NRHP-listed luxury inn · becomes machine-readable rather than a detail a guest only learns on arrival.
